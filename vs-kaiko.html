The core difference

On-chain DeFi and centralized exchanges are different markets with different mechanics.

Kaiko's institutional strength is in centralized exchange data: normalized, high-frequency, deeply historical. It is well-suited for compliance reporting, benchmark construction, and CEX-based derivatives analytics.

The institutional capital allocator who needs to evaluate or execute in Solana's on-chain DEX ecosystem — Raydium, Orca, Jupiter — is operating in a market that Kaiko was not built to cover. The question is not which provider is better. It is which provider covers the intelligence you need for the decision you're making.

When to use which.

Use Kaiko when you need:

  • →Deep historical CEX price and volume data for backtesting or compliance
  • →Regulatory-grade reference rates and index methodology support
  • →Normalized, auditable CEX trade data across 400+ exchanges
  • →CEX-based derivatives analytics (perpetuals, funding rates)
  • →Pre-2020 historical data for benchmark construction

Use Birdeye Peak when you need:

  • →Real-time Solana DEX liquidity depth for institutional-size trades
  • →On-chain pre-trade due diligence before token allocation
  • →Smart money wallet tracking and whale flow signals
  • →Token holder concentration and unlock event monitoring
  • →On-chain evidence for IC reports and LP reporting

Many institutional desks run both. Kaiko handles CEX market data and compliance requirements. Peak handles on-chain DEX intelligence and pre-trade due diligence. They cover different layers of the same investment process.
